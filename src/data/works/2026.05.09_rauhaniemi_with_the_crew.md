**Land of a Thousand Lakes.**

Finland isn't called this just for poetic beauty. The name comes from reality. There are almost **187,888 lakes** across the country, and many of them stretch far beyond what the eye can see. More than **10 percent** of Finland's total area is covered by water, and that blue calmness shapes the rhythm of life here.

A few days ago, I visited **Näsijärvi** in Rauhaniemi with friends. After winter, when the ice finally melts, Finnish nature feels like it breathes again. The frozen surface turns back into clear blue water, and the sky rests softly on it like a reflection of peace.

We took a tram and a bus from home, then started walking along the lakeside. Tall pine trees, gentle sunlight, and the quiet sound of water created a kind of silence that feels alive. Standing on the big rocks by the shore, looking at the endless blue, time seemed to slow down. The water was so clear that even the stones beneath were visible.

You can rent kayaks here and explore the lake from the inside. We met an American traveler who shared stories about exploring and fishing around the lake. Even small conversations with strangers feel like part of the experience in a place like this.

**Näsijärvi** is the largest lake in the Tampere region, covering about **256 square kilometres**. The Tammerkoski rapids flow from this lake through the heart of Tampere, and the city itself grew around that water.

Moments like these, with friends, nature, and quietness, remind me why Finland truly deserves the name _Land of a Thousand Lakes_.  
A country where water is not just part of nature, but part of life.
